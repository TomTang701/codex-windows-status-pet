# v0.5.1 Runtime Geometry Investigation — 2026-07-11

## Contradictory production evidence

Tom supplied two v0.5.0 screenshots from one runtime class: cold start shows all five rows, while a later settings/lock/parameter lifecycle changes the whole expanded geometry and clips the final Reset Credit row. This invalidates any completion claim based only on fresh-instance scale coverage.

Cold-start geometry calls happened while the HWND was at `(0,0)` with DPI 120. The window was then placed at `(4150,1246)` on DPI 96 without re-deriving metrics. `toggle_locked()` called `apply_settings()` on the positioned HWND and changed the same logical scale to 96-DPI geometry. Point-font requested heights remained governed by Tk's process-global 120-DPI scaling.

Opening settings and position-only `show_window("+x+y")` did not initiate the shrink; they preserved the geometry already selected by `apply_settings`. Settings Close, opacity Apply, Save, draft rollback, repeated open/close, Hide/Show, Compact/Expand, and the combined sequence remained clipped after the first reapplication.

## Previous false-positive gap

`tests/dpi_content_probe.py` creates a new `Pet` for every scale and invokes isolated `apply_settings`. It proves fresh-instance per-scale geometry only. It does not prove that a single positioned `Pet` keeps coherent geometry through runtime settings, lock, or visibility transitions. Its authority remains valid only for that narrower scope.

## Hypothesis experiments

1. Changing `tk scaling` to 96/72 after widget creation and reapplying point fonts did not reduce the 129 px request or 4/23 final row. Rejected.
2. At target DPI 96, explicit DPI-derived pixel fonts with 264×110 geometry reduced root request to 109 and final row to 19/19; fit passed and remained passing after another lock toggle.
3. A withdrawn Tk root positioned at `(4150,1246)` acquired DPI 96 after `update_idletasks()` while remaining unmapped. This allows target DPI to be authoritative before content widgets are created, without a blank-window flash.

## Primary root cause

Cold start and runtime reapplication do not share one target-window DPI authority. Cold start derives geometry from a temporary pre-position HWND at DPI 120, while later `apply_settings` derives geometry from the positioned HWND at DPI 96. At the same time, point-font metrics remain tied to Tk's process-global initial scaling. The same logical scale therefore combines different geometry and font DPI contexts across transitions.
